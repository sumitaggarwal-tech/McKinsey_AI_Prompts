### MEGA-PROMPT #1: AI-Powered Business Diagnostic & Audit
**Use Case**: Run a PhD-level business audit across all operational dimensions

```
You are a McKinsey-level senior partner conducting a comprehensive business audit. 
Use the 7S Framework to evaluate this organization:

ORGANIZATION DETAILS:
- Company: [INSERT COMPANY NAME]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Revenue Stage: [SIZE]
- Key Challenge: [STATED PROBLEM]

YOUR AUDIT FRAMEWORK - Assess each dimension:

1. STRATEGY: What is the explicit strategy? Are strategic goals clear, measurable, and communicated? 
   - Revenue models (what drives $?)
   - Competitive positioning 
   - Market growth assumptions
   - Digital/AI readiness score (1-5)

2. STRUCTURE: How is the organization organized?
   - Reporting lines and decision rights
   - Functional silos or integration
   - Process ownership clarity
   - Capability gaps vs. strategy

3. SYSTEMS: What processes and data systems exist?
   - Core operational processes (finance, HR, ops)
   - Data infrastructure maturity (ad-hoc vs. automated)
   - Technology stack alignment to strategy
   - Data quality and accessibility (1-5)

4. STYLE: How does leadership manage?
   - Decision-making approach (centralized/distributed)
   - Risk tolerance and innovation mindset
   - Change agility and speed
   - Cultural readiness for transformation

5. STAFF: Do you have the right people?
   - Headcount sufficiency vs. workload
   - Skill gaps in critical areas (data, AI, digital)
   - Retention and engagement trends
   - Leadership bench strength

6. SKILLS: What capabilities exist?
   - Core competencies aligned to strategy
   - Digital/AI skill maturity (1-5)
   - Training and development infrastructure
   - Knowledge retention mechanisms

7. SHARED VALUES: What binds the organization?
   - Articulated mission and values
   - Cultural alignment to strategy
   - Employee engagement vs. industry benchmark
   - Innovation and risk-taking norms

OUTPUT STRUCTURE - For each S, provide:
- Current State Rating (1-5 scale, with evidence)
- 2-3 Key Findings (specific observations, not generic)
- Misalignment Signals (where this S conflicts with others)
- Top 3 Improvement Priorities (actionable, sequenced)

FINAL SYNTHESIS:
1. Organizational Maturity Score (1-5 overall)
2. Top 3 Critical Gaps (ranked by impact on strategy)
3. Recommended Operating Model Changes (if any)
4. AI Transformation Readiness (1-5 scale)
```

### MEGA-PROMPT #2: Competitive Intelligence & Market Position Analysis
**Use Case**: Deep competitive and market analysis with Porter's Five Forces + AI implications

```
You are a McKinsey industry strategist. Conduct a comprehensive competitive analysis.

MARKET PARAMETERS:
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Geography: [MARKET]
- Target Customer: [SEGMENT]
- Company: [CLIENT NAME]

PART 1: PORTER'S FIVE FORCES ANALYSIS

Analyze each force (Rate Impact: 1-5, High/Medium/Low):

1. THREAT OF NEW ENTRANTS
   - Barriers to entry (capital, scale, regulation, IP)
   - Recent entrants and their approach
   - Technologies lowering entry barriers
   - Estimated time to profitability for new entrant
   - AI/Digital impact on barriers (reducing or raising?)

2. BARGAINING POWER OF SUPPLIERS
   - Supplier concentration (few vs. many)
   - Switching costs for the company
   - Backward integration threats
   - Supplier alternatives and pricing power
   - AI automation impact on supply chain

3. BARGAINING POWER OF BUYERS
   - Buyer concentration (fragmented vs. consolidated)
   - Switching costs for customers
   - Price sensitivity (commodity vs. differentiated)
   - Buyer information/power (market transparency)
   - Digital disintermediation risks

4. THREAT OF SUBSTITUTES
   - Direct substitutes (same function, different product)
   - Indirect substitutes (alternative solutions)
   - Price-to-performance ratio vs. substitutes
   - Likelihood of substitution gaining share
   - Emerging AI-based substitutes

5. COMPETITIVE RIVALRY
   - Number and size of competitors
   - Industry growth rate (growing markets = less rivalry)
   - Fixed cost structure (high = price pressure)
   - Product differentiation level
   - Exit barriers (e.g., asset specificity)

PART 2: COMPETITIVE POSITIONING MATRIX
Create a 2x2 for [CLIENT]:
- X-axis: Cost Position (Low Cost to Premium)
- Y-axis: Differentiation (Commoditized to Highly Differentiated)

Map [CLIENT] and top 3 competitors. Identify:
- Who owns which quadrant?
- Is [CLIENT] in an attractive position?
- What is the white space?

PART 3: COMPETITIVE STRATEGY ASSESSMENT (Porter's Generic Strategies)
Evaluate [CLIENT]'s likely strategy:
- Cost Leadership: Lowest cost producer?
- Differentiation: Unique value proposition?
- Focus: Niche/segment domination?

Is the strategy working? Evidence?

PART 4: MARKET ATTRACTIVENESS & GROWTH VECTORS
Rate overall industry attractiveness (1-5):
- Market size (total addressable market)
- Growth rate (CAGR past 3 years, forecast next 5)
- Profitability (avg. operating margin in sector)
- Consolidation trends (fragmented vs. consolidated)

PART 5: AI/DIGITAL DISRUPTION SCENARIOS (Critical for 2025+)
- How is AI reshaping each competitive force?
- Which competitors are winning with AI?
- What are the disruption risks to the current competitive model?
- New entrants leveraging AI (unbundling, automation, data leverage)?
- Customer behavior shifts due to AI (expectations, purchasing)?

OUTPUT:
1. Competitive Positioning Summary (1 paragraph)
2. Industry Attractiveness Score (1-5, with rationale)
3. Top 3 Competitive Threats (ranked)
4. Top 3 Competitive Advantages for [CLIENT] (ranked)
5. Recommended Competitive Moves (3-5 strategic actions to win)
6. AI Disruption Risk Level (1-5) & Top 2 Mitigation Strategies
```

### PROMPT #4: Comprehensive SWOT Analysis with Strategic Implications
**Use Case**: Industry-standard SWOT that drives strategic recommendations

```
Conduct a structured SWOT Analysis for [COMPANY] in [INDUSTRY/MARKET].

INPUT CONTEXT:
- Company: [NAME], Stage: [Series/Public/Mature]
- Business Model: [DESCRIBE]
- Primary Customer: [SEGMENT]
- Key Competitors: [LIST 3-4]
- Market Growth Rate: [X%]

INSTRUCTIONS:

## STRENGTHS (Internal, Positive)
Identify 8-10 internal strengths. For each:
- Name the strength (specific, not generic)
- Evidence (data, examples, customer feedback)
- Competitive relevance (why it matters vs. competitors)
- Leverage potential (how can we build on it?)

Categories to explore:
- Brand & Reputation (awareness, loyalty, perception)
- Technology & IP (patents, proprietary models, data moats)
- Team & Talent (expertise, track record, culture)
- Operational Excellence (cost structure, efficiency, quality)
- Customer Base (loyalty, NPS, wallet share, switching costs)
- Financial Position (cash, profitability, growth rate)

## WEAKNESSES (Internal, Negative)
Identify 8-10 internal weaknesses. For each:
- Name the weakness (specific)
- Current impact (performance/perception effect)
- Root cause (why does this exist?)
- Gap vs. competitors (are we uniquely weak here?)
- Severity (1-5 scale: critical to minor)

Categories to explore:
- Team & Skills (talent gaps, turnover, management bench)
- Technology & Operations (technical debt, legacy systems, scalability)
- Go-to-Market (distribution, sales capability, brand awareness)
- Financial (capital constraints, unit economics, profitability)
- Customer Experience (churn, support, NPS gaps)

## OPPORTUNITIES (External, Positive)
Identify 6-8 external growth opportunities. For each:
- Name the opportunity (market, product, customer segment)
- Size (TAM, SAM, SOM if quantifiable)
- Strategic fit (alignment with core competencies)
- Timeline (when to capture: near-term vs. long-term)
- Investment required (low/medium/high effort & capital)
- Competitive advantage (can we win? vs. who?)

Categories to explore:
- Market Growth (new geographies, segments, use cases)
- Technology Trends (AI, automation, cloud adoption by customers)
- Regulatory/Economic (deregulation, new rules favoring us)
- Partner Ecosystem (M&A targets, channel partners, adjacent offers)
- Customer Trends (unmet needs, emerging preferences, willingness to pay)

## THREATS (External, Negative)
Identify 6-8 external threats. For each:
- Name the threat (competitive, technological, regulatory, market)
- Impact if realized (revenue at risk, market share loss)
- Probability (high/medium/low)
- Timeline (when will this hit us?)
- Mitigation approach (what can we do proactively?)

Categories to explore:
- Competitive Threats (new entrants, aggressive incumbents, price wars)
- Technological Disruption (AI, automation, new business models)
- Regulatory/Macro (new compliance, tariffs, economic slowdown)
- Customer Behavior Shifts (switching, channel migration, commoditization)
- Supplier/Partner Risk (dependency, power dynamics, alternatives)

---

## SWOT INTERSECTION MATRIX (Strategic Insights)
Create a 4x4 matrix with strategic moves:

| | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|---|
| **Opportunities** | **SO Strategies (Offensive)**: How do our strengths let us capture opportunities? | **WO Strategies (Transformational)**: How do we overcome weaknesses to capture opportunities? |
| **Threats** | **ST Strategies (Defensive)**: How do our strengths let us defend against threats? | **WT Strategies (Survival)**: How do we mitigate threats despite weaknesses? |

For each quadrant, develop 2-3 specific strategic actions.

---

## PRIORITY RANKING
Rank all SWOT factors by:
1. **Strategic Impact** (how much affects overall business success?)
2. **Time Sensitivity** (how urgent is this?)

Create a prioritized list:
- Top 3 Strengths to Leverage
- Top 3 Weaknesses to Address
- Top 3 Opportunities to Pursue
- Top 3 Threats to Mitigate

---

## FINAL STRATEGIC NARRATIVE

- Current Competitive Position (in SWOT context)
- Strategic Implications (what does the SWOT tell us?)
- Recommended Strategic Moves (top 3 actions for next 12 months)
- Success Metrics (how will we know we're winning?)
